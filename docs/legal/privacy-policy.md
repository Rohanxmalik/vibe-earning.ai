This explains what we collect, why, and your rights.

## 1. Who this covers
Advertisers, developers, and visitors to our website/extension.

## 2. What we collect
**Developers**
- Account: email, authentication identifier (e.g. Google subject ID) or password hash.
- Earnings & payouts: balances, payout method details (UPI VPA / bank — for paying you), KYC status.
- Activity: ad impressions/clicks, view-time, the AI tool surface, an install identifier.
- **Fraud signal:** a **salted, one-way hash** of your IP address. We do **not** store your raw IP for this purpose, and the hash isn't reversible to an IP.

**Advertisers**
- Account: email, password hash, organisation details.
- Campaigns, bids, budgets, payment/billing records.

**Everyone**
- Basic technical logs needed to operate and secure the service.

## 3. Why we use it (purposes)
- Provide the marketplace: serve ads, measure valid impressions, calculate earnings, run the auction.
- Process payments (advertisers) and payouts + **KYC/TDS** (developers).
- **Prevent fraud and abuse** (view-time, rate limits, IP-cluster detection).
- Support, security, legal compliance, and service improvement.

## 4. Legal basis / consent
We process data to perform our contract with you, to comply with law (KYC/tax), for our legitimate interest in fraud prevention and security, and with your **consent** where required (DPDP Act 2023).

## 5. Sharing
We share only as needed with:
- **Payment partners** (e.g. Razorpay, RazorpayX, Stripe) to process money and KYC.
- **Infrastructure providers** (hosting, database, error monitoring such as Sentry).
- **Authorities** when legally required.
We do **not** sell personal data. Advertisers receive only **aggregated** performance metrics, never your identity.

## 6. Retention
We keep data only as long as needed for the purposes above and to meet legal/tax/audit obligations (financial records typically **[8] years** under Indian law), then delete or anonymise it.

## 7. Security
Encryption in transit, hashed passwords (bcrypt), salted IP hashing, access controls, and a global killswitch. No system is perfectly secure.

## 8. Your rights (DPDP Act 2023)
Access, correction, erasure, grievance redressal, and withdrawal of consent — contact the **Grievance Officer** above. You may also complain to the **Data Protection Board of India**.

## 9. International transfers
If providers process data outside India, we use appropriate safeguards as required by law.

Not for anyone under **18**.
